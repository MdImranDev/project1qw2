NEET

NEET eligibility criteria 2020 has been prescribed by the National Testing Agency in accordance with the Indian Medical Council Act-1956 and the Dentists Act-1948 as amended in 2018. The National Eligibility cum Entrance Test (NEET) is an entrance test conducted for admission to various medical and allied courses, including MBBS, BDS and AYUSH. Thus, the eligibility criteria of NEET 2020 sets up a basic minimum qualification of a candidate to apply for the said courses. NTA has removed the upper age limit clause from NEET 2020 eligibility criteria thus enabling aspirants older than 25 years to apply for it. The cap on the number of attempts has also been removed from the eligibility criteria of NEET 2020.  All those who wish to apply for the national entrance test, are advised to check their eligibility before filling the NEET application form 2020. If a candidate fails to do so and is found ineligible, his/her admission will be cancelled. Read the complete article to know more about the NEET 2020 eligibility criteria.

Basic NEET Eligibility Criteria 2020

  • Candidate must have attained the age of 17 years at the time of admission or will attain the same by December 31, 2020
  • There is no upper age limit to appear for NEET 2020. The clause had been challenged in the High Court of Delhi and the verdict is pending
  • Candidates older than 25 years of age can appear for NEET 2020 provisionally
  • Indian nationals/ Overseas Citizens of India (OCIs) who wish to pursue MBBS/ BDS from a foreign university also have to qualify NEET 2020

Eligibility criteria of NEET 2020: Qualifying Marks

  • Candidates must have passed in Physics, Chemistry, Biology/Biotechnology and English individually in the qualifying examination.
  • Candidates belonging to the general category must have scored a minimum of 50% in the qualifying examination.
  • Candidates belonging to Scheduled Caste/ Scheduled Tribes/ Other Backward should have a minimum score of 40% in the qualifying examination.
  • Candidates who belong to the General category and are applying under the Persons with Disability (PWD) Quota, must have a minimum score of 45% marks in the qualifying examination.

NEET Eligibility Criteria 2020 – Number of Attempts

The maximum number of attempts for the NEET exam has not been directed by the NTA. Thus, allowing a candidate to appear for NEET as many times as they want to.

NEET Eligibility Criteria 2020 for Candidates Belonging to the UT of J&K/ Ladakh

Candidates belonging to the UT of J&K/ Ladakh are not eligible to apply for the 15% All India Quota seats and can only apply for the seats of Deemed Universities, ESIC and AFMC. 

NEET Eligibility Criteria 2020 for Candidates Applying for State/ Deemed/ Central/ ESIC/ AFMC Seats

  • Indian Nationals, NRIs, OCIs and PIOs are eligible to apply for these seats
  • Foreign nationals should confirm their eligibility before applying for NEET 2020

Eligibility Criteria of NEET 2020 for Candidates Applying for 15% All India Quota Seats

  • Indian Nationals, NRIs, OCIs, PIOs and foreign nationals are eligible to apply for these seats
